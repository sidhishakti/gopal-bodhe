Gopal Bodhe

Author, photographer, and pioneer of aerial storytelling.

Born on 9 July 1948, Shri Gopal Bodhe served the Indian Navy for 32 years before devoting himself full-time to photography and authorship. What began as a practical skill for livelihood at the age of ten evolved into a lifelong pursuit of visual storytelling. He trained in advanced photography through international workshops and went on to explore diverse genres including nature, wildlife, aerial and infrared photography.

He is widely recognised as a pioneer of aerial photography in India. His topographic and environmental documentation includes aerial coverage of more than 8,500 km of India’s coastline and the Ladakh region, creating a rare visual archive of seascapes, forts, lighthouses and ancient trade routes. His 62-foot panoramic print of Mumbai’s Marine Drive, displayed at the World Photo Fair (1999) and now at the INS Vikrant Museum, has been recorded in the Limca Book of Records.

Beyond his published books, Shri Bodhe has conducted numerous workshops and slide shows for WWF-India, Bombay Natural History Society, Mumbai University and other institutions, using photography as a tool for environmental and heritage awareness. His exhibitions on Mumbai, Goa, Maharashtra and wildlife have attracted hundreds of thousands of visitors, often in collaboration with government and conservation bodies.

His photographic work has been appreciated by former Presidents and Prime Ministers of India, senior defence leadership and international dignitaries, and has earned several awards from national and municipal bodies. Through his images and books, he continues to inspire people to look at India’s natural and cultural landscape with renewed respect and responsibility.
